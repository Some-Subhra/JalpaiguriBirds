Zuluk-Lungthung-Kupup: The Pangolakha WLS Complex

Zuluk: The Silk Route

The Place


The Pangolakha WLS is basically the continuation of the Neora Valley NP into Sikkim which streaches further into Bhutan to form Torsa Strict Nature Reserve. Though the actual Pangolakha WLS is very much above the 7000ft mark, yet its access being from the foot hills makes this area a Bird-Watcher’s paradise.

Contiguous habitat from 2000ft near Rongli to 12000ft around Lungthung create a compact area of unique avian diversity from Green Magpies to White-Winged Grosbeaks. This region is home to almost all the eastern Himalayan specialities.


Bird Life


The birdlife in this region is very diverse, mainly due to the contiguity of the habitat right from the foothills all way up to the Chumbi Valley in Tibet. Around Rongli and Lingtam one will be getting a blend of high and low altitude varieties depending on the season. Some of the worth mentioning species in this part are White-Crested Laughingthrush, White-Throated Laughingthrush, Striated Laughingthrush,Common Green Magpie, Brown Dipper, Coral-Billed Scimitar Babbler, Rusty-Cheeked Scimitar Babbler, Scarlet Finch, Grey-throated Babbler, Spotted Wren Babbler,White-Spectacled Warbler, Scarlet & Long Billed Minivet, Grey Wagtail and so on.

Himalayan Monal


The habitat between Padamchen and Zuluk is a mixture of typical broad-leaved and Maling bamboo patches. This area is a favourite haunt of Black-throated, Brown & Fulvous Parrotbills, apart from the commoners like Bar-throated Siva, Rufous-Capped Babler, Green-tailed Sunbird, Chestnut-bellied Rockthrush etc. Other sought after Species include Spotted Laughingthrush, Rufous-throated Wren Babbler, Golden Bush Robin, White-Browed Bush Robin and Rufous-Bellied Eagle.Rarities like Satyr-Tragopan and Red Panda has also been observed along this stretch.

Great Parrotbill

From Zuluk it is an almost 4000ft climb to the Lungthung top beyond the Thambiviewpoint. This is the Himalayan Monal Territory. This is probably the best place to observe the Himalayan Monal anywhere in the Eastern Himalayas. The bamboo patches between Zulukand Lungthung provide the best chances for the rare Great Parrotbill apart from the Brown one. Among the other sought after species, the Slender-Billed scimitar Babbler, Fire-Tailed Myzornis, Fire-tailed Sunbird, Dark-Rumped and Dark-Breasted Rosefinch, White-Browed Rosefinch, Rufous-breasted Accentor are the more prominent ones.

“Blood Pheasant” the state bird of Sikkim has been uncommonly observed at the Lungthug top area. Apart from the bird life, the region is also good for mammals like Brown Goral, Red Fox and Pikas.

Vegetation


Because of the vast altitudinal variation , Birdwatchers can actually enjoy a diverse variety of vegetation between Rongli and Kupup. At the base of the hills around Rongli and Lingtam one experiences the sub-tropical moist-deciduous forests. Around Padamchen, with the increase in altitude this vegetation becomes more of the broad-leaved type.
Further beyond, towards Zuluk the Broad-leaved forest is broken at major areas by Maling Bamboo patches . This vegetation trend continues almost up to the Thambi View point near Lungthung. From Lungthung top, all way up to Kupup the vegetation is of Sub-alpine and Alpine type.

Get In


Nearest Airport: Bagdogra – 195 km away.
Nearest Rail Station: Alipurduar Junction / New Alipurduar – 37 km.
Nearest Town/ City: Alipurduar – 35 km.
By Road: NH 31 is the main highway 25 km away. One can hire a car from Alipurduar or Rajabhatkhawa, 20 km away.

Keep In Mind


Since this region is situated very close to India’s international border with China, the zone is restricted and one needs special permit for such a visit. Permits are available at Gangtok and Rongli Police Station. Till date, this region is not open to foreigners. It is better to visit the area in a group through local operators who offer tailored packages. For serious bird-watchers, a minimum of five days is recommended to cover the whole stretch. It rains without notice, so having the raincover with one is advisable for the whole of the year. A visitor can also cross Kupup and complete the full circle to reach Gantok via Tsongmo Lake.
